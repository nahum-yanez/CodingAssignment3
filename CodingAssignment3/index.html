Yerba Mate

Yerba mate offers a unique beverage experience that provides a smooth energy boost and can improve focus thanks to this naturally occurring trifecta: caffeine (also found in coffee), theobromine (also found in chocolate), and polyphenols (also found in green tea).

In South America, yerba mate is enjoyed either hot or cold depending on the region. It’s traditionally served in a hollowed-out gourd and sipped through a filtered metal straw known as a bombilla. Guayakí makes traditional dried yerba mate that’s perfect for gourds and lattes, as well as refreshing cans and bottles.

Recipe cited: Guayakí, C. (2023, October 4). What is Yerba Mate? Yerba Madre. https://yerbamadre.com/blogs/the-gourd-circle/what-is-yerba-mate

Ingredients list

  • Yerba Mate Leaves: The main ingredient is the yerba mate leaf (Ilex paraguariensis).
  • Natural Caffeine: Yerba mate contains natural caffeine (amount varies by preparation).
  • Polyphenols and Antioxidants: Includes xanthines, saponins, and flavonoids.
  • Essential Nutrients: Vitamins and minerals such as vitamin C, B vitamins, magnesium, potassium, and manganese.
  • Natural Flavours: Some varieties include added natural flavors like mint, lemon, or hibiscus.

Directions for hot Yerba Mate

  • Fill a cup about ⅓ full of loose yerba mate leaf.
  • Next, tilt the cup until all the yerba is leaning against one side of the cup and fill the vacant space with cold water.
  • Once the cool water has been absorbed, fill the cup with hot water (around 70–80°C).

Directions for cold Yerba Mate (Tereré)

  • Yerba mate can also be consumed cold.
  • Replace the hot water with cold water. This method is called “tereré” and is common in Paraguay.
  • If you don’t have a gourd and bombilla, you can brew it in a French press.
  • Alternative: replace the tea leaves with desiccated coconut, and water with milk for a sweet drink.
