Yerba Mate Drink

About Yerba Mate

Yerba mate offers a unique beverage experience that provides a smooth energy boost and can improve focus thanks to this naturally occurring trifecta: caffeine (also found in coffee), theobromine (also found in chocolate), and polyphenols (also found in green tea).

In South America, yerba mate is enjoyed either hot or cold depending on the region. It’s traditionally served in a hollowed-out gourd and sipped through a filtered metal straw known as a bombilla. Guayakí makes traditional dried yerba mate that’s perfect for gourds and lattes, as well as refreshing cans and bottles.

Ingredients List

  • Yerba Mate Leaves: The main ingredient is the yerba mate leaf (Ilex paraguariensis). These leaves come from an evergreen shrub native to South America. Yerba mate leaves are harvested, dried, and often cut into pieces for preparation.
  • Natural Caffeine: Yerba mate contains natural caffeine. Its caffeine provides gentle, sustained stimulation.
  • Polyphenols and Antioxidants: Yerba mate leaves are rich in polyphenols and antioxidants, including xanthines, saponins, and flavonoids.
  • Essential Nutrients: Yerba mate can contain vitamins and minerals like vitamin C, B vitamins, magnesium, potassium, and manganese.
  • Natural Flavours: Some varieties include added natural flavors (mint, lemon, hibiscus, etc.).

Directions for Hot Yerba Mate

  1. Fill a cup about ⅓ full of loose yerba mate leaf.
  2. Tilt the cup until the yerba is leaning against one side and fill the empty space with cold water. Let the yerba absorb the water.
  3. Once the cool water has been absorbed, fill the cup with hot water (around 70–80°C).
  4. If you don’t want to use a thermometer, stop the kettle before it bubbles or let boiled water sit about 5 minutes to cool.

Directions for Cold Yerba Mate (Tereré)

  1. Yerba mate can also be consumed cold.
  2. Replace the hot water with cold water. This method is called “Tereré” and is common in Paraguay.
  3. If you don’t have a gourd and bombilla, you can brew yerba mate in a French press.
  4. Optional alternative: replace tea leaves with desiccated coconut and water with milk for a sweeter drink.
